Short Instructions:
  1. Try to ask about central, relevant aspects of the situation and anything that could plausibly have happened before or after.
  2. Please do not formulate the same question again with different words, every question should be unique.
  3. Formulate only questions that can be answered with a couple of words, i.e. no complex explanation or elaboration should be required.
  4. To refer to the persons acting in the situation, please use "they", since you don't know for sure whether it is a man, woman, or multiple persons.
Imagine you told a child a story about a person visiting a restaurant. Try to find out if it understood everything correctly and ask questions!

Type 1: Asking for time points/time spans

  1. When did they order a dessert?
  2. When did they pay?
  3. How long did they wait?

Type 2: Asking for persons, locations, other content details

  1. Who cooked the food?
  2. Where did they sit?
  3. What did they order?

Type 3: Asking for reasons or for how something happened

  1. How did the food taste?
  2. Why did they order the vegetarian option?
  3. Why did they ask for a reservation?

Type 4: Free questions

  1. What did they use to flavour their food?
  2. Why did they complain about their steak?
  3. Which type of wine did they drink?
  4. How many courses did they have?
  5. What cutlery was used to cut the food?
  6. What did the waiter bring?
