Welcome to our experiment. By participating in this experiment you will contribute to a research about how people conduct everyday activities, for example, baking a cake . You will be asked at most 20 checkbox questions like
We are considering the situation that people bake a cake. Which of the following events does buying flour directly make easier or possible?
  • As the options we see a number of actions involved in baking a cake. Right below each option is an example of how people may narrate it. Please make sure to go through these examples before you decide on each option.
  • You are supposed to choose as many options as you consider directly made easier to accomplish by the action in question (here: buying flour). In case you consider none of the options appropriate, please choose "none of these".
  • In this example, "make the dough" should be chosen, because we need, among others, flour, to make a dough. "buying flour" will have the flour prepared for us, hence leading us one step closer to "make the dough".
  • For a similar reason, "measure ingredients" should be chosen: we need to have the flour bought before all ingredients could be measured.
  • "put cake into oven" should not be chosen: "buying flour" does not directly help us to "put cake into oven", as what is relevant is the cake instead of the flour.
  • Finally, "choose a recipe" should not be chosen, since "buying flour" does not help us to "choose a recipe".
